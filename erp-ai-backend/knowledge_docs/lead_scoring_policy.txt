Lead Scoring Policy — CRM AI System
=====================================

PURPOSE
Leads must be scored consistently so the sales team focuses effort on the highest-value opportunities.

SCORING TIERS

High Priority:
- Budget explicitly stated as greater than $5,000
- Customer has an urgent timeline (e.g., "need this within 2 weeks", "ASAP", "before end of month")
- Returning / repeat customer regardless of budget
- Executive or C-level contact initiating the lead
- Inbound lead from a paid referral or trusted partner channel

Medium Priority:
- Stated budget between $1,000 and $5,000
- Timeline mentioned but not urgent (e.g., "sometime next quarter")
- Previous interaction but no confirmed purchase history
- Warm referral from an existing customer
- Lead has asked multiple detailed questions indicating genuine interest

Low Priority:
- Budget below $1,000 or no budget mentioned
- No clear timeline provided
- First contact with vague or exploratory intent
- Lead originated from a cold outreach campaign with no engagement history

SPECIAL RULES
- Always treat repeat customers as Medium or higher, regardless of current budget.
- If a lead has both a low budget AND an urgent timeline, escalate to Medium and flag for manager review.
- Leads with incomplete contact information default to Low until verified.
- Re-evaluate priority after every significant interaction or new information.

PRIORITY ASSIGNMENT PROCESS
1. Read the lead notes and any attached emails.
2. Identify budget signals, timeline indicators, and customer relationship history.
3. Apply the tier definitions above.
4. Record the assigned priority and the reasoning in the lead notes field.
5. Notify the assigned sales rep of any High priority assignment immediately.
